Information about New Zealand

New Zealand, also known as Aotearoa, is a land of breathtaking beauty and adventure. The country is made up of two main islands, each with its own unique landscapes and attractions. From snow-capped mountains and lush rainforests to golden beaches and rolling farmlands, New Zealand is a paradise for nature lovers and thrill-seekers alike. The culture is a rich blend of Māori and European influences, and the people, known as Kiwis, are famous for their friendliness and hospitality.

      Popular attractions

      • Milford Sound: Experience spectacular fjords, towering cliffs, and cascading waterfalls. Milford Sound is often called the "eighth wonder of the world" and is best explored by boat or kayak. Keep an eye out for dolphins, seals, and penguins that inhabit these pristine waters.
      • Rotorua: See geothermal wonders and immerse yourself in Māori culture. Rotorua is famous for its bubbling mud pools, geysers, and natural hot springs. Visitors can also enjoy traditional Māori performances and sample food cooked in a hāngi, an earth oven.
      • Mount Cook: Enjoy the view over New Zealand's tallest mountain, Aoraki/Mount Cook. The surrounding national park offers excellent hiking trails, stargazing opportunities, and the chance to see glaciers up close. It's a must-visit for outdoor enthusiasts and photographers.

      Exciting activities

      • Hiking in the National Park: New Zealand is famous for its Great Walks, such as the Milford Track and Tongariro Alpine Crossing. These well-maintained trails take you through some of the country's most stunning scenery, from alpine peaks to ancient forests. Hiking is a fantastic way to experience the natural beauty and tranquility of New Zealand.
      • Visit lakes: The country is dotted with beautiful lakes, including Lake Taupo and Lake Tekapo. These lakes are perfect for swimming, kayaking, fishing, or simply relaxing by the shore. Lake Tekapo is also known for its clear night skies, making it a top spot for stargazing.
      • Taste local favorites: New Zealand's cuisine is a delicious mix of fresh seafood, tender lamb, and seasonal produce. Don't miss trying green-lipped mussels, crayfish, and the famous pavlova dessert. The country is also renowned for its world-class wines, especially Sauvignon Blanc and Pinot Noir.

      Tips

      Get the most out of your visit by following our insider tips:

      • Best time to visit: November to April is the warmest and sunniest period, ideal for outdoor activities and exploring the beaches. However, each season has its own charm, winter is perfect for skiing, while spring and autumn offer mild weather and fewer crowds.
      • Hidden gems: Discover places like Wanaka, a tranquil lakeside town surrounded by mountains, and Abel Tasman National Park, famous for its golden beaches and coastal hiking trails. These spots are less crowded but just as beautiful as the more famous destinations.
      • Transport: Renting a car or campervan gives you the freedom to explore at your own pace and reach remote areas. New Zealand's roads are well-maintained and scenic, making road trips a popular choice. For those who prefer not to drive, guided tours and public transport are also available.
